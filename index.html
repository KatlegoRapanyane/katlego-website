🌸 About Me

👋🏾 Dumelang! I’m Katlego Rapanyane, a proudly Pedi woman from the vibrant kasi streets of South Africa, walking boldly in both my culture and my calling in tech.

💡 Click the button for a fun fact about me!

I'm a final-year IT student and research assistant, passionate about digital transformation, data analysis, and empowering others — especially young women — to claim their space in the tech world.

Raised in Dev-rede (Ga-Mabelele) , I learned the value of community, resilience, and creativity from an early age. These lessons shape how I approach coding and problem-solving today.

As a future data analyst, I use tech to make a difference. I believe in the power of data to tell stories, drive decisions, and create opportunities — especially for young women and people from underrepresented backgrounds.

Tech isn't just about code — it’s about community, culture, and creating change. Being Pedi is more than my heritage; it’s the rhythm in how I work, the values I live by, and the perspective I bring to every project.

Botho • Hlompho • Maatla (Humanity • Respect • Strength)

💬 My Story

From De-Vrede (Ga-Mabelebele) to the digital world, my journey is about storytelling — through code and culture. I grew up with Sepedi proverbs like "Tšhila e tšhweu e tšwa leganong" (Beauty rises from struggle).

Today, I write clean code with the same care my grandmother weaved her doeks. Debugging is like untangling oral stories passed down under the stars. 🌌

Every tech solution I create beats with the spirit of Ubuntu — it’s not just for me, it’s for us.

I believe technology can preserve culture. Having been inspired and empowered by diverse cultures, I am committed to uplifting young women from my community by teaching coding through training that celebrates and preserves Sepedi storytelling and cultural heritage

🧶 Sepedi Symbols

Our beadwork and patterns tell stories — each color and shape has meaning.
Red for bravery, blue for peace, yellow for wealth, green for growth, white for purity.

📚 Sepedi Language

Did you know? “Kgoga ge e fofa ke ye ntsho, mebala re e bona ge e hwile.”
Translation: The hawk appears dark in flight; its true colors are revealed only at rest.
Just like quantum particles, whose hidden states are only revealed when measured, this Pedi wisdom reminds us that true potential often lies beneath the surface. That’s why quantum computers can solve complex puzzles regular computers can’t — they reveal hidden possibilities! 🦅⚛️ #QuantumPower #PediWisdom
